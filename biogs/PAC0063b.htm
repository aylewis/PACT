Program Rekindling (2013 - )

From
2013
Website
https://www.bangarra.com.au/rekindling

Summary

Rekindling's vision is to inspire pride, kinship and a sense of strength in young Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islanders through a series of dance residencies with secondary school-aged students. Participants research and gather stories with guidance from Elders within their communities, developing skills to produce thought provoking performances and community events.

Details

Sidney Saltner, Bangarra alumni and Youth Program Director describes Rekindling as being "a safe space for students to come in and explore who they are [...] I think one of the main things is that some of the kids probably think that the program is dance training and it's not about that. It's about everyone coming in to learn, and dance is just a tool that we use to express this."

Saltner says: "The program is designed to reconnect elders and youth in terms of gathering stories and knowledge from the local area [...] Some of the kids don't have the confidence of speaking in front of people or even saying their name and this creates space for them to be able to do that."

Rekindling runs for two to three months in each community and Bangarra later revisits the students to deliver additional workshops and invite participants to bring a friend. This element of the program encourages skill sharing and assists in maintaining relationships with those who have completed the program.

Rekindling was established in 2013, and by the end of 2017 had been delivered in 25 communities across five states of Australia, with more than 450 young people taking part in the program.
