Project Echange {Exchange}/Post{e}

Summary

An ongoing series of performative art installations by artist Marc Losier, based on alteration and exchange of archival source material.

Skip to

  • Published Resources

Details

An examination of the nature of collecting, through the guise of a non-monetary art market, every artwork in the Ã‰change(Exchange)/Post(e) reflects a unique aspect of localized history appropriated from documents and materials found in public archives and libraries in Toronto.

The value of each work is based on the individualized relationship between the collector and the image. Each work can be acquired during the exhibition through the exchange of written stories, memories or an expressed interpretation of the piece. As the art works are acquired, they are replaced with written responses that are collected, catalogued, and archived.

Losier describes the process of exchange as "...a way of opening up the project to the public, where usually archives are closed to the public, or repressed, or restricted in some way. It [is] about flipping that around and making them really open and accessible and kind of more informal."

Exhibition sites to date include: Sudbury (2014), North Bay (2015), Dawson City (2016) and Corner Brook (2017).
