Organisation Parramatta Female Factory Precinct Memory Project (2013 - )

From
2013
Parramatta, New South Wales, Australia
Website
https://www.pffpmemory.org.au

Summary

Parramatta Female Factory Precinct Memory Project is a base for social history and contemporary art, established in 2013 with a vision to transform this once inaccessible site into a place of shared cultural heritage so that historical injustices are not repeated. Memory Projects connect past to present and memory to action by bringing together artists, historians, researchers and people with a direct experience to document, record and interpret the history, heritage and legacy of institutional confinement of women and children in Australia, acknowledging the significance of institutional sites of incarceration in the Australian narrative for the Forgotten Australians, Stolen Generations and convict descendants.

Details

The aims of Parramatta Female Factory Precint Memory Project are to:

- Deliver a diverse program of workshops, events, exhibitions, presentations, tours and publications that stimulate dialogue about contemporary questions of social justice and human rights associated with the institutionalisation of women, children and people with mental or cognitive disabilities.

- Foster agency for people who experienced institutional confinement as children in their journey of recovery and self determination.

- Provide opportunities for former occupants to determine how they want to be remembered and how the site may be utilised in the future.

- Encourage community engagement with this site, its histories and its people.

- Promote awareness of the significance of these sites for Forgotten Australians, Stolen Generations and the descendants of convict women.

- Represent and inform government and key stakeholders on contemporary issues historically associated with these institutional sites including poverty, gender discrimination, inequality, domestic violence, child abuse, forced migration, wrongful detention and racism.

- Work towards establishing a permanent memorial, commemorative space and Keeping Place at these sites.

- Work with institutions of higher learning and like minded organisations in the pursuit of common goals.
