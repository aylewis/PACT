Exhibition Lost Childhoods: Voices of Santa Cruz County Foster Youth and the Foster Youth Museum (2017)

From
7 July 2017
To
31 December 2017
Website
https://santacruzmah.org/2016/lost-childhoods-july-7-2017-december-31-2017/

Summary

An exhibition about foster youth, created with over 100 local foster youth, artists, and youth advocates. The exhibition is a platform for dialogue and action, revealing what happens in foster care - and concrete ways to support child welfare in the documented context.

Details

As of 2017, there are 60,000 youth in California's foster care system. They struggle to avoid homelessness, prison, and social stigma. At the core of the Lost Childhoods exhibition is a collection of photographic portraits by Ray Bussolari, as well as artifacts from the Foster Youth Museum. The exhibition also includes works by former foster youth who are on the MAH advisory committee, as well as by local artists. "We really chose artists based on how much they wanted the youths' voices to shine versus their own," explains the MAH community engagement director, Stacey Garcia.

Garcia describes the collaboration: "We are not experts in what foster youth have gone through, what they want to share. We know how to make an exhibition, but we don't know how to tell their story. They do."
